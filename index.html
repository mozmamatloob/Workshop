Step 01: Data Acquisition

World Development Indicators Analysis

This project utilizes a comprehensive dataset from the World Bank, spanning from 1990 to 2020. It includes over 30 key indicators such as GDP, life expectancy, and internet penetration across all global regions.

Project Roadmap:

  • Data Cleaning: Handling missing values via group-wise mean imputation.
  • Feature Understanding: Analyzing distributions and relationships.
  • Trend Identification: Observing global shifts in technology and wealth.
Step 02: Distribution

GDP Per Capita Distribution

Analysis: As seen in our initial outlier check, the data is highly right-skewed. Most nations cluster at the lower end of the income spectrum, with a few high-income outliers significantly extending the tail.

Step 03: Time-Series Trends

Global GDP vs. Internet Access

Analysis: This dual-axis plot illustrates the simultaneous rise of average global wealth and digital connectivity. Notice the sharp inflection point in internet adoption after the year 2000.

Step 04: Feature Relationships

Correlation Heatmap

Analysis: Based on our heatmap, we identified strong positive correlations between Electricity Access and Life Expectancy (0.84), while Mortality Rates show a strong negative correlation with wealth indicators.

Step 05: Bivariate Analysis

Internet Access vs. GDP Per Capita

Analysis: This scatter plot confirms that higher internet penetration is a strong predictor of economic prosperity. Each dot represents a country, color-coded by year to show the upward progression over time.

Step 06: Categorical Analysis

Life Expectancy by Income Group

Analysis: Categorizing nations into four income brackets reveals a stark disparity. High-income nations boast an average life expectancy exceeding 80 years, while low-income regions remain significantly lower.
